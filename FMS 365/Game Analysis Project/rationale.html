Game Selection & Rationale

Why Life Is Strange: True Colors is a strong choice for analyzing interactivity, player agency, emotion, and narrative design.

Why This Game?

Life Is Strange: True Colors is a narrative-driven adventure game developed by Deck Nine Games. The story follows Alex Chen, a young woman with the supernatural ability to sense, absorb, and sometimes influence the emotions of others.

Game Selection and Rationale

I chose Life Is Strange: True Colors for this project because it strongly connects to the major themes we have discussed in this course, especially interactivity, agency, emotional storytelling, and the relationship between player and character. Unlike games that focus mainly on combat or speed, this game centers on dialogue, relationships, exploration, and meaningful decisions.

What makes this game especially interesting is the way Alex’s empathy power works as both a gameplay mechanic and a narrative device. Players do not just watch Alex understand other people’s emotions—they actively participate in that process. This creates a stronger sense of connection between the player, the story, and the character herself.

I also selected this game because it provides strong examples of how visual design, color, music, and atmosphere can support storytelling. The town of Haven Springs feels intimate and emotionally charged, which makes the player’s choices feel more personal. Because of this, the game is a strong example of how narrative and design can work together to create an immersive experience.

“True Colors stands out because it makes emotion interactive, allowing the player to experience empathy as both a story element and a form of gameplay.”

Alex Chen is the center of the game’s emotional and narrative design.
