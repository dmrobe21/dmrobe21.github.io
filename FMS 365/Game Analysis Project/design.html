Game Design & Elements

Exploring how visual style, structure, and gameplay systems shape the experience of Life Is Strange: True Colors.

How the Game is Built

Life Is Strange: True Colors is a narrative adventure game that focuses on storytelling, relationships, and emotional interaction rather than combat or fast-paced mechanics.

Game Design and Elements

The game falls into the narrative-driven adventure genre, where player choices and story progression are the core focus. Instead of traditional objectives like defeating enemies or completing missions, players are encouraged to explore the environment, interact with characters, and make decisions that influence the story.

One of the most important design elements is the structure of the game itself. It follows an episodic format, allowing the story to unfold gradually while giving players time to reflect on their choices. This pacing supports the emotional tone of the game and helps build stronger connections between the player and the characters.

The visual design plays a major role in shaping the experience. The town of Haven Springs is designed to feel warm, detailed, and immersive. The use of lighting, color, and environment helps communicate mood and emotion, often reinforcing what characters are feeling without needing explicit dialogue.

Overall, the game’s design prioritizes atmosphere, storytelling, and emotional engagement. By focusing on these elements, it creates a different type of gameplay experience—one that is more reflective and personal than action-oriented.

“The design of True Colors shows that gameplay does not need to be fast or competitive to be engaging—emotional connection and atmosphere can be just as powerful.”

Haven Springs is designed to feel intimate, immersive, and emotionally rich.
