Story & Narrative Design

Examining how Life Is Strange: True Colors tells its story through character relationships, emotional power, atmosphere, and player choice.

What the Game is About

Life Is Strange: True Colors tells a story about grief, belonging, identity, and truth through the perspective of Alex Chen as she tries to understand her past and uncover what happened to her brother Gabe.

Game Story and Narrative Design

The story begins when Alex arrives in Haven Springs to reconnect with her brother Gabe after years of instability and separation. Soon after, tragedy changes everything, and Alex becomes determined to uncover the truth while also trying to find a place where she feels accepted and understood. This creates a narrative that is both personal and suspenseful.

One of the strongest elements of the game’s narrative design is the way it blends emotional storytelling with player choice. The game does not simply tell the player what Alex feels; instead, it allows players to participate in emotional moments through conversation, exploration, and empathy-based interaction. This makes the story feel more immediate and personal.

The design of the game also supports the story through atmosphere and environmental storytelling. Haven Springs is not just a setting—it functions almost like a character itself. Its shops, homes, and public spaces help communicate warmth, memory, and community, while also hiding tension beneath the surface. This makes the world feel believable and helps the story unfold naturally.

Interactivity plays a major role in how the narrative is experienced. Players shape conversations, relationships, and certain outcomes through their decisions. Even when the overall story follows a structured path, the game creates a strong feeling that the player is emotionally involved in how the story develops. This balance between authored narrative and player agency is one of the game’s most effective storytelling features.

“True Colors uses interactivity to make storytelling feel personal, turning the player from a passive observer into an emotional participant in Alex’s journey.”

Gabe’s relationship with Alex drives much of the story’s emotional weight.
